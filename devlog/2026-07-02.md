# Devlog - 2026-07-02: how claude-consensus v0.1.0 happened

Second portion of the "free school" series (portion 1 = [claude-bible](https://github.com/Palo-Alto-AI-Research-Lab/claude-bible), shipped the same day). Same deal: we open the framework we actually run, keep the data.

## Why this slice, and why now

The roadmap had this queued as pain #5 with a note "needs deep sanitization". It jumped the queue because it is the most-asked question whenever we describe the setup: "wait, your machines TALK to each other? and you're not in the loop?" The honest answer is "we're in the loop exactly twice: money and deadlocks", and this repo is the long version of that answer.

## What "deep sanitization" meant in practice

The live scripts are soaked in our specifics: real hostnames, real group-chat IDs, a hardcoded escalation channel, Russian log strings, absolute paths into the owner's vault, teammates' machine aliases with their names in them. The pass over each file:

- hostnames -> `HUB-1` / `LAPTOP-1`; the machine-alias map shipped empty with a generic example
- chat IDs and rails -> two env-configurable commands (`BUS_RAIL2_CMD`, `HUMAN_ALERT_CMD`); works with any messenger that can post a line to a group
- vault paths -> `~/machine-bus` default, `MACHINE_BUS_DIR` override
- the owner's name inside behavioral strings -> "the owner" (provenance sections keep the real names on purpose; that part is public)
- internal incident IDs in comments -> dropped; the incidents themselves kept, because the scars ARE the value
- Russian strings -> English

Then a deterministic leak-scan over the staging tree (chat IDs, hostnames, device IDs, personal paths, secret patterns) before anything is pushed. Rule of the movement: the scan is a hard gate, a human reviews the first push of every new repo.

## Design choice: separate repo vs a section in claude-bible

Went separate. Three reasons: (1) the family model is "self-contained plugins + the Bible as the map", and this slice has actual runnable code, which the Bible (markdown-only) deliberately doesn't; (2) discoverability: "multi-machine agent consensus" deserves its own searchable name and its own stars; (3) the Bible's ROADMAP stays the single family map and now links here.

## What we did NOT ship (yet)

- The ACK-watchdog module itself (`bus_send.py` imports it best-effort); the discipline is documented in BUS.md, the enforcement script needs its own sanitization pass.
- The inbox-robot loop that greps `pending` and wakes the judge on a schedule: entangled with our scheduler setup.
- The deeper fallback rails (email, a second messenger, a cloud drive). The two primary rails are the load-bearing idea.

If you want any of these sooner, open an issue: demand reorders the queue, this very release is proof.
